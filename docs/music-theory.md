## Common Keys by Genre

- **Techno/House**: Am, Cm, Dm, Fm
- **Lo-fi**: Eb, Db, Gb, Ab (flat keys feel warm)
- **Jazz**: Bb, Eb, F, Ab (horn-friendly)
- **DnB**: Em, Am, Dm (dark minor)
- **Trap**: Fm, Cm, Bbm (dark, cinematic)
- **Synthwave**: Am, Em, Cm (80s minor keys)
- **Trance**: Am, Cm, Dm (euphoric minor keys, some major for uplifting)

## Chord Progressions by Genre

### Pop/Rock
- I-V-vi-IV (C G Am F)
- vi-IV-I-V (Am F C G)

### Jazz
- ii-V-I (Dm7 G7 Cmaj7)
- I-vi-ii-V turnaround (Cmaj7 Am7 Dm7 G7)
- iii-vi-ii-V (Em7 Am7 Dm7 G7)

### Lo-fi Hip Hop
- ii7-V7-Imaj7 (Dm7 G7 Cmaj7)
- Imaj7-vi7-ii7-V7 (Cmaj7 Am7 Dm7 G7)
- im7-iv7-VII7-IIImaj7 (Cm7 Fm7 Bb7 Ebmaj7)

### House
- im-iv-VII-III (Am Dm G C)
- im-VI-VII-III (Am F G C)

### Techno
- Single chord drones or two-chord oscillation
- im-bVII (Am G) or im-iv (Am Dm)

### Ambient
- Open voicings, sus chords, free modal movement
- Imaj7-IVmaj7, Isus2-Vsus4

### Trap
- im-bVI-bVII (Fm Db Eb)
- im-iv-bVI-bVII (Fm Bbm Db Eb)

### Synthwave
- im-bVII-bVI-bVII (Am G F G)
- im-iv-bVI-bVII (Am Dm F G)

### Trance
- im-bVII-bVI-V (Am G F E) — classic uplifting progression
- im-iv-bVI-bVII (Am Dm F G)

## Genre Sub-styles

### House
- **Deep house** (120-124): Soulful, jazzy chords, warm pads. Dorian/mixolydian. More reverb, less busy.
- **Tech house** (124-128): Minimal + groovy, percussive. Less harmony, more rhythmic variation.
- **Progressive house** (126-132): Building layers, long filter sweeps, evolving arpeggios.
- **Acid house** (124-130): 303 bass, squelchy resonant filter, raw energy.

### Techno
- **Berlin** (128-136): Raw, dark, industrial. Distorted kicks, phrygian, sparse arrangement.
- **Detroit** (125-135): Futuristic, soulful. Lush pads, strings, more melodic. Dorian/major.
- **Minimal** (125-132): Very few elements, micro-variations. 2-3 layers, lots of subtlety.
- **Industrial** (130-145): Harsh, noisy. Distortion, noise layers, metallic percussion.

### DnB
- **Liquid** (172-176): Smooth, melodic, soulful. Jazz chords, warm pads, rolling bass. Dorian/major.
- **Neurofunk** (174-178): Clinical, glitchy bass design. Complex Reese bass, sparse, dark.
- **Jungle** (160-170): Amen breaks, ragga influences, chopped samples.
- **Jump-up** (172-176): Punchy, simple, dancefloor. Strong snares, wobbly bass.

### Hip Hop
- **Boom bap** (85-95): Classic kicks/snares, vinyl crackle, jazz samples. Lo-fi feel.
- **Trap** (130-170): 808 bass, rolling hi-hats, dark minor keys.
- **Drill** (138-145): Dark, sliding 808 bass, minor keys, sparse melodies.
- **Phonk** (130-140): Memphis-style, chopped vocals, cowbells, heavy lo-fi processing.

### Jazz
- **Bebop** (180-280): Fast changes, complex lines, ii-V-I. Walking bass, ride cymbal.
- **Modal** (100-140): One scale extended, open improvisation. Dorian/mixolydian.
- **Smooth** (90-120): Accessible, polished. Electric piano, gentle bass.
- **Fusion** (100-160): Rock/funk elements, odd meters, more aggressive.

### Additional Genres
- **Synthwave** (80-118): 80s nostalgia, saw arps, gated reverb, lush pads. Minor keys.
- **Vaporwave** (60-90): Slowed samples, heavy reverb, chopped and screwed aesthetic.
- **IDM/Glitch** (100-160): Complex rhythms, euclidean patterns, probability, experimental sound design.
- **Trip-hop** (70-100): Downtempo, cinematic, vinyl drums + deep bass + moody pads.
- **UK Garage / 2-step** (130-140): Shuffled beats, skippy hats, pitched vocals, warm bass.
- **Trance** (128-150): Uplifting arpeggios, long buildups, euphoric drops. Progressive/uplifting/psy sub-styles.
- **Dubstep** (138-142): Heavy bass drops, wobble bass, half-time drum patterns.
